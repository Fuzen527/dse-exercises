The King in chess is able to move to any square that shares an edge or corner with the current square in one move.

Now you have a chessboard with no other pieces and you want to move your King piece from one square to another. Calculate the minimum number of steps required.

Input

The input consists of two lines.
The first line is the starting square in algebraic notation.
The second line is the destination square in the same format.

Output

Output a single integer: the minimum number of steps required to move the King pieces from the starting square to the destination square.
